# Philosophy

Before using or contributing to this best practices guide, it's a good idea to understand the
philosophy behind the project, in order to make sure it aligns with your goals.
This page explains the principles anchored in the AWS Networking Best Practices guide, and
discusses the [conventions] used in this documentation.

  [conventions]: conventions.md

## Design principles

- __Focus on Real-World Use Cases__: Center the content around practical use cases drawn from field experiences rather than individual services. Emphasize networking scenarios that are frequently encountered in the real world, offering insights that resonate with the daily challenges of AWS architects and users. 

- __Prioritize Strong Fundamentals__: Concentrate on fundamental networking principles that are critical for robust architecture and design. Avoid speculative or cutting-edge possibilities, instead reinforcing the core concepts that underpin successful network strategies.

- __IPv6 as a Core Element__: Treat IPv6 as a primary consideration, integrating it into examples, diagrams, and discussions as the standard. Ensure that all networking best practices include IPv6 as a fundamental component, reflecting its importance in modern networks.

- __Technical Precision and Clarity__: Maintain a high level of technical accuracy, using clear, concise language tailored for a knowledgeable audience. Provide links to existing validated content where appropriate, ensuring readers have access to deeper resources without duplicating existing information.
